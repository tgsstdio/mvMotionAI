  2. Detailed Entity Description

    1. mvBody

      2. This object in the mvMotionAI library is the most important as it represents moving items such as people, cars, planes etc. There are currently three types of mvBodies existing in this system.

      1. Particle

        2. This body is for moving objects that do not have a central direction ('facing' a single direction), so are omnidirectional at all times . Examples are balls and particles.

      2. Vehicle

        3. This body represents objects that move along a single direction. Turning is required to alter its intended path. Typical examples are cars, boats and bicycles.

      3. Dual Type

        4. Dual type covers objects which act like vehicles for some tasks such as path following and particle-like movement like quick evasion.

    2. mvForce

      3. This class represents environmental forces which act on bodies; similar to gravity and wind.

      There are also subsets of mvForces implemented in the system.

      1. Forces

        2. Forces are important as dependant on the mass of the body. According to the formula, force = mass/acceleration or acceleration = force / mass, lighter objects are more affected than heavier ones. Forces can be either directional or uniform.

      2. Acceleration or gravity

        3. Gravity in a simulation is often computed as a uniform acceleration in a single direction. Here mvForces' acceleration can represent environment acceleration as uniform or directional.

      3. Shifts

        4. We refer to environmental elements that apply constant velocity to bodies as shifts. Example of shifts in action is wind in sailboat simulation, as a general approximation.
